The Monty Hall problem

Let's assume a TV show where we are given a choice between three doors. Behind one door there is a car, behind the other two there are goats. We can choose a door, at which point the host (who knows what is behind every door) opens a different door, showing a goat.

He then offers the choice of switching our selection of doors, or sticking with the one already chosen. The question is: Should we switch the door chosen, or stay with the one we first chose?

The alternating scenario variant calculates the odds of winning if one alternates between staying or switching door selection in subsequent games
